Research

My research focuses on enabling robots to efficiently learn and adapt to new tasks with minimal supervision through foundation model-based approaches. I am interested in developing scalable learning frameworks that can leverage pre-trained models and structured guidance to achieve rapid task acquisition without requiring extensive demonstrations for each new scenario. This involves exploring how robots can generalize learned capabilities across diverse environments and tasks, while maintaining sample efficiency and robustness in real-world settings. Ultimately, I aim to advance autonomous robot learning methods that can bridge the gap between the rich knowledge embedded in foundation models and the practical demands of real-world deployment, moving toward more adaptable and capable robotic systems. ReWiND: Language-Guided Rewards Teach Robot Policies without New Demonstrations

ReWiND enables sample-efficient adaptation to new tasks by training a language-conditioned reward model and policy from a small set of demonstrations to learn new tasks without additional per-task demonstrations. We beat baselines by 2X in simulation and improve real-world pre-trained policies by 5X in just 1 hour.
